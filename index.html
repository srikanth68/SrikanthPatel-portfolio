01 — About
Who I Am
I believe great software isn't just built — it's architected. My work sits at the intersection of clean engineering, thoughtful design, and forward-looking tech.

I started out as a teaching assistant, explaining code to eager students. Somewhere along the way, I fell in love with the art of structuring systems — not just writing code, but designing the blueprints that make teams ship faster and software last longer.

Over 14 years I've worn many hats — developer, analyst, senior engineer, architect — all within the .NET ecosystem. Today, I lead architectural decisions for the State of Maine's IT office while staying curious about emerging tech like generative AI and how it can sharpen the way we design and deliver software.
